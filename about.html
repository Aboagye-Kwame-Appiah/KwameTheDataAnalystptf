Data Analyst · QA Background · 7+ Projects

My career started in Quality Assurance — enforcing FDA regulations, ISO 9001, and ISO 17025 laboratory standards in operational environments where data accuracy wasn't a preference, it was a legal requirement. I learned to build systems where the margin of error is a risk, not a footnote.

Through a rigorous Data Analytics programme, I channelled that discipline into the full analytics stack: cleaning and transforming data in Python, querying datasets in SQL, building dynamic models in Excel, and designing executive dashboards in Power BI and Tableau. The regulatory mindset became an analytical advantage.

Today, the projects I build span e-commerce revenue tracking, manufacturing optimisation, tourism analytics, and B2B performance intelligence. Every output is designed with one question in mind: what decision does this enable?

I'm actively seeking roles where data directly shapes operational decisions — where an analyst isn't just reporting what happened, but helping determine what should happen next.

What I Believe
Principles That Shape
How I Work

Decision-Driven Analysis

Every analysis starts with a decision that needs to be made. If I can't articulate what action the data enables, the question isn't defined well enough yet.

Traceable Truth

Data conclusions must be reproducible and defensible. I document methodology, flag assumptions, and validate outputs — not as extras, but as standard practice.

📐
Clarity Over Complexity

Complex models nobody can interpret create no value. I optimise for the clearest communication of the most relevant insight.

Measurable Outcomes

Analytics value is measured by the decisions it enables and the results those decisions produce. I hold my own work to that standard.

The best dashboards are built with stakeholders, not for them. I ask the questions that reveal the actual business need beneath the surface request.

Continuous Sharpening
